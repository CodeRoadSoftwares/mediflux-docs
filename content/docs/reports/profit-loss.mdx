The Profit & Loss (P&L) statement — also called an income statement — is one of the most important financial reports for your pharmacy. It summarizes your revenue, costs, and expenses over a specific period, showing you whether your pharmacy is operating at a profit or a loss.

MediFlux generates P&L statements automatically from your ledger data, breaking down the numbers into categories that are meaningful for pharmacy operations. You can generate P&L reports for any date range, compare periods side by side, and drill down into individual line items to understand what's driving your financial results.

## Understanding the P&L Structure

A MediFlux P&L statement follows this structure:

```
Revenue
├── Pharmacy Sales (Rx)
├── OTC Sales
├── Medical Device Sales
└── Other Revenue
─────────────────────────────
= Gross Revenue

Less: Returns & Allowances
─────────────────────────────
= Net Revenue

Less: Cost of Goods Sold
├── COGS - Pharmaceuticals
├── COGS - OTC Products
└── COGS - Medical Devices
─────────────────────────────
= Gross Profit

Less: Operating Expenses
├── Salaries & Wages
├── Rent & Utilities
├── Insurance
├── Marketing
├── Depreciation
└── Other Operating Expenses
─────────────────────────────
= Operating Income (EBIT)

Less: Interest & Taxes
─────────────────────────────
= Net Income
```

## Generating a P&L Report

### From the Dashboard

Navigate to **Accounting → Reports → Profit & Loss**. Select your date range, location (or "All Locations" for consolidated), and any comparison options. Click **Generate Report** to view the results instantly.

### Via the API

```typescript title="Example: Generate P&L report via API"
const report = await fetch("https://api.mediflux.io/v1/reports/profit-loss", {
  method: "POST",
  headers: {
    "Content-Type": "application/json",
    Authorization: "Bearer your-api-key",
  },
  body: JSON.stringify({
    start_date: "2024-01-01",
    end_date: "2024-03-31",
    location_id: "all",
    compare_to: "previous_period",
    include_categories: true,
  }),
});

const data = await report.json();
```

## Key Metrics to Monitor

<Callout type="info">
  MediFlux calculates these key performance indicators automatically on every P&L report. Use them to benchmark your pharmacy's performance against industry standards.
</Callout>

### Gross Margin

Your gross margin measures the percentage of revenue remaining after deducting the cost of goods sold. For independent pharmacies, a healthy gross margin typically ranges from 25% to 35%.

```
Gross Margin = (Net Revenue - COGS) / Net Revenue × 100
```

### Operating Margin

Operating margin shows the percentage of revenue remaining after all operating expenses are paid. This indicates how efficiently your pharmacy converts revenue into profit.

```
Operating Margin = Operating Income / Net Revenue × 100
```

### COGS Ratio

The cost of goods sold ratio indicates what percentage of your revenue goes toward purchasing inventory. A lower ratio means higher profitability per sale.

| Category         | Industry Average | Target          |
| ---------------- | ---------------- | --------------- |
| Pharmaceuticals  | 65–75%           | Below 68%       |
| OTC Products     | 50–60%           | Below 55%       |
| Medical Devices  | 55–65%           | Below 60%       |
| Overall          | 60–70%           | Below 65%       |

## Period Comparison

MediFlux supports several comparison options to help you identify trends:

- **Previous Period** — Compare to the immediately preceding period of the same length
- **Same Period Last Year** — Year-over-year comparison to account for seasonality
- **Budget** — Compare actual results against your budget (if configured)
- **Custom** — Compare against any arbitrary date range

<Callout type="warning">
  When comparing periods, ensure you're comparing equal-length time ranges. Comparing a 31-day month to a 28-day month without adjustment can lead to misleading conclusions. MediFlux normalizes daily averages in its comparison calculations to account for this.
</Callout>

## Drilling Down

Every line item in the P&L report is clickable in the dashboard, allowing you to drill down into the underlying transactions. For example, clicking on "Pharmacy Sales" shows you the individual sales transactions that make up that revenue figure, with filters for date, product, and customer.

This drill-down capability makes the P&L report a powerful diagnostic tool — when you notice an unexpected number, you can trace it back to the specific transactions that caused it.

## Scheduling Reports

Automate P&L report delivery to stakeholders:

```typescript title="Example: Schedule monthly P&L report"
await fetch("https://api.mediflux.io/v1/reports/schedules", {
  method: "POST",
  headers: {
    "Content-Type": "application/json",
    Authorization: "Bearer your-api-key",
  },
  body: JSON.stringify({
    report_type: "profit_loss",
    frequency: "monthly",
    day_of_month: 5,
    recipients: ["owner@healthfirst.com", "accountant@healthfirst.com"],
    format: "pdf",
    compare_to: "previous_period",
    locations: "all",
  }),
});
```

<Callout type="success">
  Scheduled P&L reports are generated on the specified day and emailed as PDF attachments. Recipients don't need MediFlux accounts to receive them.
</Callout>
